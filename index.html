About My Thesis

My doctoral research, titled “Emotion-Aware Natural Language Processing for Human-Centric AI Systems”, explores the integration of emotional intelligence into natural language processing models.

The motivation behind this work stems from the increasing role of AI systems in education, healthcare, customer support, and social platforms—areas where understanding human emotions is crucial. Traditional NLP systems process text effectively but fail to capture the emotional subtleties of language. This gap often results in robotic, insensitive, or even misleading responses.

Through my research, I have:

1. Designed deep learning models capable of identifying emotions such as happiness, sadness, anger, fear, and surprise in text.

2. Developed context-aware emotion embeddings that go beyond simple sentiment polarity.

3. Proposed a human-centric AI framework that enhances empathy in chatbots, digital assistants, and therapeutic applications.

This work aims to bridge the gap between linguistic accuracy and emotional sensitivity, paving the way for more compassionate AI systems.

Research Highlights

Introduction

🔹 Defined the research problem of emotion misinterpretation in NLP systems.

🔹 Outlined objectives: improving emotional understanding in AI for real-world use.

Literature Review

🔹 Critically analyzed over 200 papers across psychology, linguistics, and computer science.

🔹 Identified limitations of existing sentiment analysis and emotion classification methods.

Methodology

🔹Proposed a hybrid model combining transformers (BERT/XLNet) with emotion lexicons.

🔹Introduced an Emotion-Enriched Dataset with over 1 million labeled sentences across 6 emotion categories.

Results

🔹 Achieved 92% accuracy in multi-class emotion detection—outperforming baseline models.

🔹 Demonstrated real-world applications in AI tutors, counseling chatbots, and patient support systems.
